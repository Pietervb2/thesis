\chapter{Results}
\label{chapter:Results}
This section presents the results of the constrained Bayesian optimization applied to a full-day simulation compared with the defined benchmark simulation. Three tolerance values, $\alpha = \beta \in \{0.05, 0.15, 0.25\}$ are considered, each applied to simulations using the four heat demand profiles introduced in Section~\ref{sec::heatdemand}. Before presenting the control results, the simulation settings, the ranges of the control inputs, and the fixed values of two control inputs are described.

\section{Simulation settings}
Besides the constants presented in Section~\ref{sec::physicalConstants}, the following simulation parameters are applied. The ambient temperature is set constant at 20\,°C, and the initial temperature of the water and the pipes is set equal to this ambient temperature. Furthermore, the maximum pump pressure is set at 50 kPa.

The network used for the simulations is similar to the network used by Eneco for its in-house steady-state model of the heat distribution within a DCS project. The simulated network represents a 23-floor building, with the floors spaced 3 m apart. At every floor, a heat exchanger is connected to the riser at a distance of 2.5 m from the junction, using DN20 piping. The supply and return risers themselves are divided into three sections with decreasing diameter along the height of the building: DN40 for the lowest 6 floors, DN32 for the following 14 floors, and DN25 for the top 3 floors. The parameters of the four pipe types were presented in Tables~\ref{tab:pipe_dn_common} and~\ref{tab:pipe_dn_geometry}.

For the optimization, both weights $w_1$ and $w_2$ are set equal to 
$\frac{1}{4181} \cdot 10^{-3}$, thereby equally prioritizing both terms. Scaling down the weights in this manner keeps the resulting values of the cost function in a readable range. The warm-up timestep $k_w$, after which the simulation results are included in the cost function, is set equivalent to 6 hours. This duration corresponds to the time required for the sensor temperature within the thermostatic bypass valve to stabilize, as shown in Section~\ref{sec::appendixbypasstemp}.

\section{Control inputs}
While performing the optimization, the control inputs $\theta_4$ and $\theta_6$ seemed to not influence the objective function. This was indicated by the size of their respective lengthscales. For the fitting of the lengthscales the Bayesian Optimization got to vary the lengtscales 0.05 to 3 times their input range. Their lengthscales became the maximum of this, indicating the low correlation between the datasamples close to each other within this set input parameter range. 
-the range of theta4 changed per profile in order to adjust the P-band range to the size of the total heat demand so that the BO did not waste function evaluatios to explore those regions. 
- to check whether the GP correctly fitted to the objective function lanscape wrt $\theta_4$ a parameter sweep was done. Due to high amount of time these sweeps take it was only performed on Profile two just to have an indication. And also on $\theta_2$, $\theta_3$ and $\theta_5$ to compare their impact with $\theta_4$. The results are shown in Chapter \ref{chapter::appendixExtraPlots}, validating the fixation of $\theta_4$. It was chosen to be fixed at the halfway point of its range per profile.
